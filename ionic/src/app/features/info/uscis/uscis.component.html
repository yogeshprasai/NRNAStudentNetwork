Essential USCIS Forms for International Students

Your comprehensive guide to immigration documentation requirements

Form I-20

Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant Student Status

Required

The foundational document that confirms your acceptance to study at a SEVP-approved U.S. institution. This form is essential for obtaining your F-1 visa and maintaining student status.

When You Need It

Required before applying for your F-1 visa and must be maintained throughout your academic program.

Important: Must be signed by your DSO every 12 months for travel purposes. Keep your information current with your DSO.

Form DS-160

Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application

Required

The DS-160 is the Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application form required for most temporary U.S. visa applicants, including tourists (B1/B2), students (F-1), and workers (H-1B).

When You Need It

It must be completed and submitted electronically before scheduling a visa interview at a U.S. embassy or consulate.

More Info about DS-160
Form I-901

SEVIS Fee Payment

Required

Payment required to register in the Student and Exchange Visitor Information System (SEVIS). Must be paid before your visa interview.

When to Pay

After receiving your I-20 but before your visa interview. Keep the payment receipt for your records.

More Info about I-901
Form I-765

Application for Employment Authorization

Optional

Required for Optional Practical Training (OPT) work authorization after completing your academic program.

Filing Window

For post-completion OPT: Up to 90 days before program end date and up to 60 days after completion.

Note: USCIS must receive your application within 30 days of your DSO's recommendation in SEVIS.
More Info about I-765
Form I-539

Application to Extend/Change Nonimmigrant Status

Situational

Used to extend your stay, change visa status, or reinstate student status if it has lapsed.

When to File

Submit well before your current status expires. For reinstatement, file as soon as possible after falling out of status.

More Info about I-539
Form I-94

Arrival/Departure Record

Required

Electronic record of your legal entry and authorized stay in the United States. Created automatically upon entry.

Access Instructions

Retrieve your I-94 record online through the CBP website after each entry to the United States.

Important: Always verify your I-94 information after entering the US to ensure accuracy.
